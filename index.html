The “talk me about yourself” question

Where to start? What to highlight? Well, let me walk you through my academic, professional and volunteering story first – so that you can decide what to deepen after.

We always communicate something – it can be about any topic, anytime. In my case, I decided to communicate for a living, and here I am: writing and speaking about privacy, AI, data governance.

Where it all begun: Languages, Culture and Marketing

I studied foreign languages (apart from English and Italian, I speak German, Spanish and Portuguese; French is on the way!), and international communication across Italy, Germany and Norway. My focus was marketing – including PR and advertising – and how people from different languages, places and cultures understand them.

A new product that needs to be adapted to different European or international markets? No problem – I got an analysis and strategy ready for this.

Getting started – first experiences across Europe

My first professional steps were rooted in digital communication and customer engagement. At fitvia in Wiesbaden, I gained hands-on experience in CRM, social media, communication and customer support – I could experience how targeted content and tailored interactions can shape user experiences across markets.

A few months after, a small jump to Stuttgart led me to STARTUP AUTOBAHN – time to work for an global, open innovation platform. There, I challenged myself in online/onsite event planning, communication strategy, PR, copywriting and community management, coordinating international startup–corporate collaborations. It was a fast-paced environment – great to strengthen my ability to plan, manage and communicate complex projects across borders.

Entering the world of Privacy, AI and Data Governance

AI, emerging technologies and data governance – was this the direction I wanted to take? Indeed it was. With a one-way ticket to Brussels, I joined the International Association Privacy Professionals, where I expanded my expertise in digital content creation, creative marketing and copywriting, event and customer support. I was not only exposed to the topics I was interested in, but I could also build a holistic understanding of global privacy and operations/technology frameworks through multiple trainings.

Today and Tomorrow

Today, I am building a career at the intersection of:

  • Marketing and communication
  • Privacy, AI governance and data management

For my career, I wish to grow into international project management (that is also on the way!), to lead impactful marketing and communication projects.
